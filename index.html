Active Park Assist

The Active Park Assist feature allows a vehicle to automatically park itself in either a parallel or perpendicular parking space.

Project Background

This is a group project for the class CSE 435 at Michigan State University taught by Dr. B Cheng. Our group will participate in a requirements engineering exercise with Ford Motor Company. Helping us with our project at Ford will be Eileen Davidson. Our task is to generate a Requirements Document for an Active Park Assist in-vehicle system. We are also tasked with building a website and presenting/demonstrating our finished project.

Project Description

As autonomous driving technologies advance further, many stressful driving situations can be eliminated. Parking is a situation that can be improved using technology such as high definition cameras, sensors, and autonomous driving. The Active Park Assist system will ease the stress of parking by letting the car do all of the work. The driver can interact with the Active Park Assist system with the Human Machine Interface (HMI). Once this system is activated, the HMI will 1) identify the parking spot, 2) accelerate/brake the vehicle 3) choose forward or reverse as required and 3) steer the vehicle into the parking spot. The system will use a radar/camera system to determine the vehicle position and the parking spot. The Active Park Assist system can be deactivated and interrupted at any time by the driver stepping on the brake.
